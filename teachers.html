Tyler Fleming

PE,Health Tyler Fleming is a student, teacher, mover and doer from the Great Lakes. For nearly ten years his teaching and learning has revolved around people and working on ways to help build a better world; it’s taken him from Michigan to New Mexico and back again, all the while deepening his belief in the power of people and movement. With his Bachelors in the Arts and Humanities from Michigan State University and his Masters in Secondary Education from the University of Michigan- Flint, his path to teaching P.E. and Health here in the city hasn’t been typical, and he wouldn’t have it any other way. When he’s not teaching Mr. Fleming can often be found drinking lots of coffee, lifting heavy things and spending time with his family.

Caitlin Kozak

Computer Science Caitlin Kozak has been working in Detroit Schools for the past four years. She has taught Environmental Science, Biology, and Introduction to STEM courses to students of all ages. She grew up in Southeast Michigan and attended the University of Michigan- Ann Arbor with degrees in Environmental Science and Urban Planning. Ms. Kozak did not realize she was destined to teach until she led a series of tutoring programs at various schools and community centers across Detroit. She loves to play basketball and volleyball, check the weather, and play outside with her dog and fiancee. She can't wait to meet all of her new students, teach them about the awesome opportunities that computer programming opens up, and watch Michigan dominate in football (finally)!

Andrea Lamb

Special Education Andrea Lamb has been a special education teacher for thirteen years and has worked in several states including New Jersey and Texas. She graduated from Southern Methodist University with a degree in Psychology. She went on to receive her Masters in Education from Texas Christian University with a focus in special education. She was born and raised in Texas. Outside of the classroom, she enjoys camping with her family and taking her dog to play fetch at the beach. Her goal this year is inspire kids to dream big and never give up.

Joe Downes

Science Teacher Born in East Lansing, Mr. Downes spent his earliest years in Niger before moving back to Michigan and graduating from East Lansing HS. Mr. Downes graduated with degrees in history and political science from the University of Michigan, also studying at the London School of Economics for a year. In 2013, Mr. Downes entered the United States Peace Corps and taught HIV/health education in Lesotho for two years. Following his time in Peace Corps, Mr. Downes moved to Cape Town, South Africa and continued teaching HIV/life skills education throughout sub-Saharan Africa. Outside of school, Mr. Downes loves kayaking, listening to all kinds of music and spending time with his friends.

Sheyna Mikeal

Teacher Sheyna Mikeal has been working in schools for the past 5 years. She was a math teacher in New York for 4 years and a school counselor in Detroit for one year. She graduated from Johns Hopkins University in Baltimore,Maryland with degrees in psychology, sociology and school counseling. After volunteering at Hopkins for academic admissions she realized that she needed to be on the other side of education to get students prepared to apply to college rather than admitting them. She loves to go to the east coast and spend time with her family in Baltimore and her friends in New York. This year she hopes to create an environment where students want to come to school and are excited about learning.

Voletta Bonner 4

Senior Teacher Voletta Bonner has been in working in schools all over Detroit for the past twelve years. A former engineer, she has served as a math teacher and instructional specialist. She graduated from Michigan Technological University with a degree in Mechanical Engineering, and University of Phoenix with a masters degree in Secondary Education. She realized education was her calling after taking a substitute teaching position as a favor for a friend. She is the mother of an amazing five year old daughter named Riley. She loves food and plans vacations around restaurant openings. Her goal this year is to assist all students on their quest of mathematical mastery.
